Islamabad

The Beautiful Capital - A Modern City Nestled in Natural Splendor

Discover Pakistan's Capital City

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, is a masterpiece of urban planning that seamlessly blends modern architecture with natural beauty. Built in the 1960s, this planned city is known for its wide tree-lined streets, modern buildings, and stunning backdrop of the Margalla Hills. As the political and administrative center of Pakistan, Islamabad offers visitors a unique combination of governmental grandeur, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

Pakistan Monument

A national symbol representing the unity of the Pakistani people, this flower-shaped monument offers panoramic views of Islamabad. The adjacent museum showcases Pakistan's history and culture. The monument is beautifully illuminated at night, making it a popular spot for evening visits.

Faisal Mosque

One of the largest mosques in the world, designed by Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay. Its unique contemporary design without traditional domes makes it an architectural marvel. Set against the backdrop of the Margalla Hills, it's a stunning example of modern Islamic architecture.

Margalla Hills National Park

A natural paradise offering hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and breathtaking views of the twin cities. The park is home to various species of flora and fauna. Popular trails include Trail 3 and Trail 5, perfect for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts seeking outdoor activities.

Lok Virsa Museum

Pakistan's premier folk heritage museum showcasing the country's rich cultural diversity. The museum features traditional crafts, musical instruments, costumes, and artifacts from all provinces. It's an excellent place to understand Pakistan's cultural heritage and artistic traditions.
